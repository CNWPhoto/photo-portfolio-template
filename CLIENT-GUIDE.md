# Client Guide

## Welcome to Your Website

Your website code lives in a GitHub repository that you own. This means you have full control — you can share access with any developer, and your site is never locked into a proprietary platform.

The site is built on **Astro**, an industry-standard static site framework used by teams at Google, Microsoft, and thousands of independent developers worldwide. Your content is managed through **Sanity**, a professional headless CMS trusted by major brands. Any web developer familiar with these tools can pick up and maintain your site.

## Updating Your Content

Your content is managed through **Sanity Studio** — a private dashboard that only you can access.

### Logging In

Go to `https://your-domain.com/studio` and sign in with your Sanity account. If you've forgotten your login, visit [sanity.io](https://www.sanity.io) and use "Forgot password."

### Updating Your Bio, Tagline, and Location

1. In Sanity Studio, click **Photographer** in the left sidebar.
2. Click your photographer document.
3. Edit the **Name**, **Location**, **Tagline**, **Bio**, and **Approach Text** fields.
4. Click **Publish** in the top-right corner when done.

### Adding or Removing Testimonials

1. Click **Testimonials** in the left sidebar.
2. To add: click **+ New Testimonial**, fill in the quote, client name, subject name, and optional photo, then **Publish**.
3. To remove: open the testimonial, click the `···` menu in the top-right, and select **Delete**.
4. Use the **Order** field (a number) to control the display sequence — lower numbers appear first.

### Uploading New Photos

1. Click **Gallery Images** in the left sidebar.
2. Click **+ New Gallery Image**, give it a title, choose a category, upload your photo, and set an **Order** number.
3. Click **Publish**.

Photos added to the gallery will appear in the Portfolio section of your site after the next deployment (usually within a few minutes on Vercel).

### Writing a New Blog Post

1. Click **Blog Posts** in the left sidebar.
2. Click **+ New Blog Post**.
3. Fill in the **Title** (the URL slug auto-generates from this), **Publish Date**, **Excerpt**, **Cover Image**, and **Body** (rich text editor).
4. Click **Publish** when ready to go live.
